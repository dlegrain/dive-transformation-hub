### SLIDE 5 — The 3-Phase Model
*Based on Lewin's change theory, adapted by Nguyen & Hong (2025)*

| Phase | Name | Days | Objective |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | ACTIVATION | 1-30 | Infrastructure, leadership, unfreeze habits |
| **2** | IMPLEMENTATION | 31-60 | Training, tool deployment, pedagogy |
| **3** | INSTITUTIONALIZATION | 61-90 | KPIs, governance, make it permanent |

**Strict progression**: Phase 2 will fail if Phase 1 isn't solid.
Deploying AI tools (Phase 2) without infrastructure and buy-in (Phase 1) is the #1 reason initiatives collapse.

### SLIDE 10 — The Professor Problem

> "Among academics, the 'champion' model can **backfire**."
>
> — Singh & Strzelecki (2026)

**What does NOT work with professors:**
- "Your colleague Prof. Nguyen is already using it!" → triggers displacement anxiety
- Public demos → performance pressure
- Peer pressure of any kind → counterproductive

**What WORKS:**
- Private pilot program: 3-5 volunteers, 30 days, no audience
- Risk-free experimentation: no installation, no sign-up
- Demonstrate time savings on THEIR specific tasks
- They share results only if THEY choose to

### SLIDE 13 — 4 Operational Recommendations

From the research — embed these in your plan:

**1. "Students are consultants, not guinea pigs"**
Include student feedback committees. They evaluate whether AI tools meet their learning needs.
(Nguyen & Hong, 2025)

**2. "Anticipate the Digital Divide"**
Any AI deployment must include support for disadvantaged students: scholarships, equipment loans.
Without this, AI adoption widens inequality.
(Nguyen & Hong, 2025)

**3. "Avoid the isolated gadget syndrome"**
Every tool must connect to the institution's strategic plan. No more pilot projects that never scale.
(Bravo-Jaico et al., 2025)

**4. "Reward the effort"**
Workload overload is the #1 barrier. Offer financial incentives or reduced teaching loads.
(Deacon et al., 2025)
