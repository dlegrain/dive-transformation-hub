### SLIDE 3 — Resistance Is Not the Enemy

> "Resistance is a **diagnostic tool**, not an obstacle."
>
> — Deacon et al. (2025)

When someone resists AI adoption, they're telling you something important:
- About their **identity** (am I still relevant?)
- About their **autonomy** (am I losing control?)
- About their **workload** (I can barely manage what I have now)

**Listening to resistance = understanding what needs to happen for change to succeed.**

### SLIDE 4 — The Triple Diagnostic

Instead of labeling someone as "resistant" or "supportive", we analyze through **3 complementary lenses**:

| Lens | Question | Source |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Lens 1** | HOW do they resist? | Deacon et al., 2025 |
| **Lens 2** | WHY do they resist? | Cao et al., 2026 |
| **Lens 3** | WHAT rational lever is missing? | Singh & Strzelecki, 2026 |

The combination of all 3 produces a **personalized counter-measure**.

And sometimes, the answer is: **they don't resist at all** — they're your champions.

---

### SLIDE 5 — Lens 1: HOW Do They Resist?
*Observable Behavior (Deacon et al., 2025)*

| Behavior | Description | Danger Level |
|---|---|---|
| **Supportive** | Actively supports, willing to lead | Not resistance |
| **Pronounced Refusal** | Open opposition, vocal rejection | Visible — manageable |
| **Pronounced Opposing** | Active argumentation, ideological pushback | Visible — requires dialogue |
| **Subtle Undermining** | Passive sabotage, quiet non-compliance | **Invisible — most dangerous** |
| **Subtle Avoiding** | "It's just a fad", scheduling conflicts, deflection | **Invisible — most common** |

**Key insight**: The most dangerous resistance is the one you don't see.
Open opposition is actually easier to handle — at least it's a conversation.

### SLIDE 6 — Lens 2: WHY Do They Resist?
*Root Cause — The 3 AI Anxieties (Cao et al., 2026)*

| Anxiety | Description | How to Address |
|---|---|---|
| **Learning Anxiety** | "I can't master this technology" | Structured, low-stakes workshops. Start simple. |
| **Sociotechnical Anxiety** | "The institution will leave me behind" | Create communities of practice. Show them their role. |
| **Displacement Anxiety** | "AI will replace me" | **The hardest.** Reframe AI as augmentation. Career orientation. |

**+ A special case:**

| | | |
|---|---|---|
| **Ethical Engagement** | "AI raises serious ethical questions" | **This is NOT resistance — it's an ASSET.** These people should draft your AI ethics policy. (Hong et al., 2026) |

### SLIDE 7 — The Discipline Factor

**Not all faculty react the same way** (Cao et al., 2026):

| Discipline | Reaction to AI Anxiety |
|---|---|
| **STEM** | Convert anxiety into motivation more easily. Focus on immediate research utility. |
| **Humanities & Social Sciences** | Need specific interventions to defuse the perceived threat to disciplinary traditions. Position AI as a critical analysis tool, not a replacement for intellectual work. |

This means your strategy for the Engineering department ≠ your strategy for the Literature department.

---

### SLIDE 8 — Lens 3: WHAT Rational Lever Is Missing?
*Adoption Decision (Singh & Strzelecki, 2026)*

| Missing Lever | They think... | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| **Relative Advantage** | "I don't see how AI improves MY work" | Show time savings on THEIR specific tasks. Not generic demos. |
| **Compatibility** | "It doesn't fit my workflow" | Adapt the tool to THEIR methods. Don't ask them to change for the tool. |
| **Low Complexity** | "It's too complicated" | No installation, no login, one-click access, guided tutorials. |

---

### SLIDE 9 — The Professor Exception

> "For professors: do NOT rely on social pressure or public demonstrations."
>
> — Singh & Strzelecki (2026)

Academics work in **silos**. They evaluate tools **privately**.

What does NOT work:
- "Your colleague is already using it!" → triggers displacement anxiety
- Public demos with an audience → feels like performance pressure
- The "champion" model → backfires with faculty

What WORKS:
- Risk-free, private experimentation (no installation, no sign-up)
- Immediate time savings on THEIR specific tasks
- No audience, no judgment

---

### SLIDE 10 — The Workload Reality

> "Workload overload is the **#1 barrier** to technology adoption."
>
> — Deacon et al. (2025)

No matter how good your AI tool is, if people are already drowning in work, they will not adopt it.

**Concrete actions:**
- Offer reduced teaching load for staff investing in digital transformation
- Provide financial incentives for course redesign
- Co-create the implementation — don't impose it top-down
