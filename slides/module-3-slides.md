### SLIDE 4 — Why Quick Wins Matter

Quick wins serve a critical psychological function:

- They create **visible proof** that change is possible
- They generate **momentum** before resistance calcifies
- They convert **skeptics** into curious observers
- They give **leadership** something concrete to communicate

> "Avoid the isolated gadget syndrome — 56% of universities adopt isolated initiatives without strategic alignment."
> — Bravo-Jaico et al. (2025)

Your quick win must be **aligned** with your morning's roadmap and your institutional priorities.

### SLIDE 5 — What Is "Vibe Coding"?

**Vibe Coding** = building software by describing what you want in natural language, and letting AI generate it.

You don't write code.
You describe what the tool should do.
The AI builds it.

**Tool**: Google AI Studio (free, browser-based)

**What you can build in 60 minutes:**
- A chatbot that answers student FAQ
- A quiz generator from lecture content
- A document summarizer for research papers
- A feedback tool for student writing

### SLIDE 10 — Tips for Vibe Coding Success

**DO:**
- Start with the simplest possible version
- Be specific about the user: "A Vietnamese student who..."
- Test with real examples from your institution
- Iterate — your first version will not be perfect

**DON'T:**
- Try to build something complex on your first attempt
- Worry about making it "production-ready" — this is a prototype
- Spend more than 10 minutes on a single prompt — refine and move on

**If you get stuck:** Ask the AI "What would you suggest to improve this?"
