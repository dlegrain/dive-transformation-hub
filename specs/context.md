# Seminar Context

## The Event

**DIVE Transformation Hub** — a 4-day seminar on AI adoption in Vietnamese higher education.
13–16 April 2026, Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam.

- **Mornings**: J. Parisse — Change Management (general frameworks)
- **Afternoons**: D. Legrain (Diederick) — AI adoption (hands-on, app-supported)
- **Language**: English only
- **Audience**: University leaders (rectors, vice-rectors, deans) and senior professors from Vietnamese universities
- **Goal**: Each group leaves with a concrete, science-backed 90-day AI adoption plan + Change Dashboard for their institution

## Audience Profile

- Decision-makers in Vietnamese higher education
- Mix of tech-savvy and non-technical profiles
- Institutional perspective: governance, compliance, faculty buy-in, student outcomes
- Cultural context: Vietnamese academic hierarchy, collectivist decision-making, government-driven digital transformation policies (Nguyen & Hong, 2025)
- English proficiency: functional but not native — keep UI language clear and jargon-free

## Daily Structure — Morning/Afternoon Integration

The core design principle: **each afternoon applies, deepens, and produces a tangible deliverable from the morning's frameworks.**

### Day 1 — From Theory to Diagnostic

| | Session | Key Content | Deliverable |
|--|---------|-------------|-------------|
| AM | J. Parisse | Lewin (unfreeze/change/refreeze), Kotter 8 steps, case study Saigon Tech University | Theoretical frameworks |
| PM | D. Legrain | Rogers' Diffusion, MTM 8-dimension model, institutional self-assessment | **Radar chart** (Module 1) |
| **Link** | The morning provides the "why change" frameworks; the afternoon answers "where are we now?" with a concrete diagnostic tool. |

### Day 2 — From Stakeholder Analysis to AI-Powered Persuasion

| | Session | Key Content | Deliverable |
|--|---------|-------------|-------------|
| AM | J. Parisse | PESTEL for HE, stakeholder mapping (power/interest matrix), change curve, resistance mechanisms | **Stakeholder map** |
| PM | D. Legrain | 3 anxieties (Cao Kai), 4 resistance behaviors (Deacon), 3 diffusion levers (Singh). NotebookLM/Gemini as tools to work ON stakeholder challenges. | **Resistance map + AI-generated persuasion strategies** (Module 2) |
| **Link** | The morning maps WHO resists and their power. The afternoon diagnoses WHY they resist (triple diagnostic) and uses AI tools to build persuasion arguments for the specific stakeholders identified that morning. |

### Day 3 — From Roadmap to Prototype

| | Session | Key Content | Deliverable |
|--|---------|-------------|-------------|
| AM | J. Parisse | Structuring a transformation roadmap, governance, leadership, quick wins identification, risk analysis | **Prioritized quick wins list** |
| PM | D. Legrain | 3-layer model (Nguyen & Hong), vibe coding demo + hands-on. Participants build a prototype of one quick win from the morning. | **Working prototype + solution cards** (Module 3) |
| **Link** | The morning identifies what to do first. The afternoon proves it can be done — a quick win identified at 11am becomes a working prototype by 4pm. |

### Day 4 — From Dashboard to Action Plan

| | Session | Key Content | Deliverable |
|--|---------|-------------|-------------|
| AM | J. Parisse | Measuring change, leading vs lagging indicators, building a Change Dashboard | **Change Dashboard with KPIs** |
| PM | D. Legrain | Vietnamese HE barriers (Taylor & Francis), 3-phase plan (Activate/Implement/Institutionalize), champions, KPIs aligned with morning dashboard. PDF export. | **90-day plan + Executive Report PDF** (Module 4) |
| **Link** | The morning defines how to measure success. The afternoon builds the plan and embeds those KPIs directly into it. The PDF is the combined output of 4 days. |

## Tools Taught During the Seminar

- **NotebookLM** (Day 2): Document synthesis and knowledge management. Used to analyze stakeholder challenges and generate persuasion strategies.
- **Gemini Deep Research** (Day 2): Structured briefing generation. Used alongside NotebookLM for institutional document analysis.
- **Vibe Coding / Google AI Studio** (Day 3): Building institutional tools without programming. Participants prototype a real quick win.

## Design Principles for the App

1. **Workshop tool, not standalone product** — assumes a facilitator is guiding the session
2. **Progressive disclosure** — modules unlock as the seminar progresses (Day 1 → Module 1 only, etc.)
3. **Morning-afternoon bridge** — each module can import or reference deliverables from the morning session
4. **Shareable outputs** — compare radar charts, share stakeholder maps between groups
5. **Exportable** — the final PDF compiles all 4 modules into one Executive Report
6. **Resilient** — localStorage persistence so a browser refresh doesn't lose work
7. **Accessible** — large fonts, high contrast, works on laptops and tablets
